# Scientific Data Sources Supporting UBT Analysis

This document compiles all scientific data sources, publications, and experimental collaborations relevant to testing UBT predictions.

## 1. Gravitational Wave Data

### LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA Collaboration

**Data Repository:**
- **GWOSC (Gravitational Wave Open Science Center)**: https://gwosc.org/
- **Data Format**: Strain time series, event catalogs, tutorial notebooks
- **Access**: Publicly available, no authentication required

**Key Publications:**

- Abbott, B. P., et al. (LIGO Scientific Collaboration and Virgo Collaboration). (2016). "Observation of Gravitational Waves from a Binary Black Hole Merger." *Physical Review Letters*, 116(6), 061102. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.116.061102

- Abbott, R., et al. (2021). "GWTC-3: Compact Binary Coalescences Observed by LIGO and Virgo During the Second Part of the Third Observing Run." arXiv:2111.03606. https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.03606

- Abbott, B. P., et al. (2016). "Tests of General Relativity with GW150914." *Physical Review X*, 6(4), 041015. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevX.6.041015

- Abbott, R., et al. (2023). "GWTC-3: Compact Binary Coalescences Observed by LIGO and Virgo During the Second Part of the Third Observing Run." *Physical Review X*, 13, 041039. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevX.13.041039

**Analysis Tools:**
- `gwpy`: https://gwpy.github.io/
- `PyCBC`: https://pycbc.org/
- `LALSuite`: https://lscsoft.docs.ligo.org/lalsuite/

## 2. Gamma-Ray Burst Data (Quantum Gravity)

### Fermi-LAT (Large Area Telescope)

**Data Repository:**
- **Fermi Science Support Center**: https://fermi.gsfc.nasa.gov/ssc/data/
- **Data Access**: Public, requires Fermi Science Tools
- **Data Products**: Photon lists, light curves, spectra

**Key Publications:**

- Vasileiou, V., et al. (2013). "Constraints on Lorentz Invariance Violation from Fermi-Large Area Telescope Observations of Gamma-Ray Bursts." *Physical Review D*, 87(12), 122001. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.87.122001

- Acciari, V. A., et al. (MAGIC Collaboration). (2020). "Bounds on Lorentz Invariance Violation from MAGIC Observation of GRB 190114C." *Physical Review Letters*, 125(2), 021301. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.125.021301

- Abdo, A. A., et al. (2009). "Fermi Observations of High-Energy Gamma-Ray Emission from GRB 080916C." *Science*, 323(5922), 1688-1693. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1169101

- Abdo, A. A., et al. (2009). "A limit on the variation of the speed of light arising from quantum gravity effects." *Nature*, 462(7271), 331-334. https://doi.org/10.1038/nature08574

- Ackermann, M., et al. (2014). "Fermi-LAT Observations of the Gamma-Ray Burst GRB 130427A." *Science*, 343(6166), 42-47. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1242353

**GRB Catalogs:**
- **GRB Coordinates Network**: https://gcn.gsfc.nasa.gov/
- **Swift BAT Catalog**: https://swift.gsfc.nasa.gov/results/batgrbcat/

**Analysis Tools:**
- Fermi Science Tools: https://fermi.gsfc.nasa.gov/ssc/data/analysis/

## 5. Cosmic Microwave Background

**Data Repository:**
- **Planck Legacy Archive**: https://pla.esac.esa.int/

- **Access**: Public, no authentication required

## 10. Data Usage Guidelines

### Citation Requirements
When using any of these data sources:
1. Cite the original experiment/collaboration publication
2. Acknowledge the data repository
3. Follow collaboration-specific citation policies
4. Include DOI links where available

### Access Policies
- Most astronomical/physics data is **publicly available**
- Some require registration but no institutional affiliation
- Analysis tools are generally **open source**
- Cite both data and software used in analysis

### Reproducibility
For scientific publication:
- Archive all analysis code (GitHub, Zenodo)
- Document software versions used
- Provide data provenance (exact files, versions)
- Make analysis reproducible by others
