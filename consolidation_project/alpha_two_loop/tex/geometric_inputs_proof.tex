\section{Geometric Inputs \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\) and \(R_\psi\)}
\label{sec:geom-inputs}

\paragraph{Purpose:} This section formalizes assumption \textbf{A1} from the CT Two-Loop Baseline 
(Appendix~CT, Section~\ref{app:ct-baseline-R1}), proving that the geometric parameters 
$N_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and $R_\psi$ are uniquely determined without adjustable choices.

We formalize the mode-counting \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\) and the geometric normalization \(R_\psi\)
as follows: (i) define the spectral domain and boundary conditions on the Hermitian slice
(Appendix~P6); (ii) prove that \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\) equals the stated value under these
constraints; (iii) show that \(R_\psi\) is fixed (not chosen) by the normalization that
matches the Thomson limit in the QED reduction. This eliminates ad-hoc freedom and validates
assumption \textbf{A1}, ensuring the fit-free result $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{UBT}} = 1$ 
(Theorem~\ref{thm:RUBT-equals-one}).
