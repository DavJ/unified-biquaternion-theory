## Summary Statement

The ST-1 scan found one genuine mechanism for three generations in UBT
(ψ-modes of the Θ-field via complex time), while three algebraic routes were
obstructed.  The key discriminator between UBT and Furey is:

> **If the ψ-mode conjectures (ST-3) can be proved:** "Octonions are
> sufficient but *not necessary* for three generations. UBT derives them from
> complex time, without leaving the associative algebra ℂ⊗ℍ."

> **If the ψ-mode conjectures remain unproved:** "ℂ⊗ℍ alone is insufficient
> for three generations via purely algebraic decomposition (proved in ST-2).
> The complex-time mechanism is a well-posed conjecture requiring dynamical
> input."

Either result is a publishable, precise statement about the structure of
division-algebra-based unification programmes.

## Detailed Notes

### Three-Generation Mechanism

**Furey:** Uses minimal left ideals of the action algebra of ℂ⊗𝕆.  The
octonion algebra contributes exactly three minimal left ideals, each
isomorphic to ℂ⁴ as a module.  The three generations are identified with
these three ideals.  The identification is algebraic and does not require
any dynamical input.

**UBT:** The obstruction theorem (ST-2) shows that ℂ⊗ℍ has no analogous
three-fold ideal decomposition.  The ψ-mode mechanism (ST-3) is the
unique UBT-specific alternative: the three lowest ψ-Taylor modes
Θ₀, Θ₁, Θ₂ of the field Θ(x,τ) are independent, carry identical SU(3)
charges, and are partially separated by ψ-parity.  The identification with
physical generations is conjectural at present.

### Associativity

**Furey:** The octonion algebra 𝕆 is non-associative.  This creates
well-known difficulties for quantization (no straightforward path integral,
no standard Haag-Kastler axioms).  Furey's construction must work within
specific subalgebras or quotient structures to circumvent this.

**UBT:** The algebra ℂ⊗ℍ ≅ M₂(ℂ) is associative.  All standard tools of
algebraic QFT, operator algebras, and path integrals apply without modification.
This is a structural advantage for building a complete quantum field theory.

### GR Recovery

**Furey:** The framework is formulated over flat spacetime and does not
address the recovery of general relativity.

**UBT:** In the limit ψ→0 (real time), the Θ-field equation reduces to a
linearised equation compatible with Einstein's field equations.  Full GR
is recovered as the real sector of UBT.  This makes UBT a potential
unification of GR and the Standard Model algebra.

### Complex Time

**Furey:** No imaginary-time coordinate; τ = t is real.

**UBT:** AXIOM B introduces τ = t+iψ.  This is the key structural feature
that enables the ψ-mode generation mechanism.  It also provides the
connection to GR (the ψ=0 limit) and to potential extensions like
Euclidean continuation.

## Conclusion

| Statement | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Furey's three-generation mechanism cannot be reproduced in ℂ⊗ℍ (algebraic obstruction) | **Proved** (ST-2) |
| UBT's ψ-mode mechanism produces three independent, same-charge modes | **Proved** (ST-3, §3) |
| The ψ-modes correspond to physical generations | **Conjecture** (ST-3, §4) |
| The ψ-mode mass hierarchy explains m_e ≪ m_μ ≪ m_τ | **Conjecture** (ST-3, §4) |
| UBT can replace octonions for three generations | **Conditional** on above conjectures |

The comparison unambiguously establishes that:

1. **UBT and Furey address the three-generation problem through
   structurally different mechanisms** (algebraic ideals vs. complex-time
   modes).

2. **UBT's mechanism preserves associativity** — a property lost in Furey's
   framework.

3. **The UBT mechanism is available only because of AXIOM B** (complex time),
   which has no analogue in Furey's construction.

4. **If the conjectures in ST-3 are proved**, the conclusion is:
   > "Octonions are sufficient but not necessary for three fermionic generations.
   > UBT derives three generations from complex time within the associative
   > algebra ℂ⊗ℍ."

5. **If the conjectures remain unproved**, the conclusion is still:
   > "ℂ⊗ℍ does not admit three generations via algebraic ideal decomposition
   > (proved). The ψ-mode mechanism is a well-defined, falsifiable conjecture
   > that would, if true, eliminate the need for octonions."
